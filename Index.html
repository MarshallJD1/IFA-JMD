Awareness

Bellow is an Accordion of information on some of the most common conditions people come up against. A small description with links to relevant resources.


Anxiety is a natural human response when we feel that we are under threat. It can be experienced through our thoughts, feelings and physical sensations.
It is what we feel when we are worried, tense or afraid. It is noted to consider that alot of people experience anxiety at some point in their lives. You are not alone. Follow the link for more information and tips on how to cope. Learn More


Depression is a mental health problem that is chracterised by low mood or loss in interest in things that you used to enjoy. It can affect how you feel and behave.
We can all have low mood at times in our life, but once it becomes a hinderence on our daily duties and just being ourselves: we should take action. Depression can vary in serverity and symptoms individually, it's all about finding out where you stand.
Follow the link for more information. Learn More.


Dissociation is an experience many people be familiar with. It is our minds way of disconnecting from the world around us. It is a coping mechanism that can be triggered by a traumatic event or be a learnt.
Dissociation, wether it brought on by trauma or not, is very specific to the indvidual. There are many different kinds of dissociation, and it can be a very complex experience.
You can find a list of diffent types of dissociation, coping mechanisms and more through the link.

Panic Attacks

Most panic attacks last between 5 and 15 minutes
Lets try some breathing exercises to help you through this.

  • Take a deep breath in through your nose, counting to 4.
  • Hold your breath for 4 seconds (less if you need).
  • Exhale slowly through your mouth, counting to 4.
  • Repeat this process.
  • Try to focus on your senses: Chew sweets, touch something soft
  • Remember, you are not alone.

Burnout

Burnout is a state of emotional, physical, and mental exhaustion caused by excessive and prolonged stress.
We all feel it from time to time, but if left unchecked it can lead to more serious mental health issues. Neglecting ones own health is easy to do in such a busy world, but it is costly.
Here are a few things to keep in mind - that you have no doubt heard before:

  • Get enough sleep - Screens off for an hour before bed
  • Exercise - 30 mins in the fresh air is enough
  • Take breaks - Frequent small breaks cater to a working mind
  • Set boundaries - Personally and physicaly
  • Learn to say no - You can only hope to help others if you can first, help yourself.
  • Seek support - You aren't alone
